Work Experience


Influential Speakers and Field Trips


Introduction

Being a member of the engineering academy has provided me with the opportunity to learn from a variety of experiences that go beyond the traditional classroom setting. Through numerous field trips and guest speakers, I have had the chance to gain insight of the real-world applications of engineering principles and it's impact on society. Through these opportunities I have had the opportunity to see how engineers work in diverse industries and apply their skills to solve complex problems. The engineering academy has not only provided me with incredible academic opportunities but also given me insight into the working world.



Joe Vierra

During a recent guest speaker presentation by Joe Vierra, a former CTO of several large companies and current startup expert, I gained valuable insights on the importance of speed, realism, and avoiding perfectionism in the pursuit of innovation. Mr. Vierra emphasized that speed is critical to ensure proficiency and efficiency in today's fast-paced world. He urged us to focus on building solutions as fast as possible and getting them out to customers, even if they're not perfect. By doing so, we can incorporate customer feedback to improve our ideas, rather than relying solely on our own vision of perfection. He also stressed the importance of being realistic, both in our planning and in our overall startup vision. Challenges are inevitable, and we must be flexible enough to modify our plans to overcome them. Most importantly, Mr. Vierra inspired me by emphasizing the significance of keeping the bigger picture in mind. While small details matter, it's crucial to never lose sight of our grander vision, and not to let perfectionism hinder our progress.



Cesar Nicola

Cesar Nicola was a guest speaker whose presentation was focussed on his insights regarding managing teams using Agile/Scrum methodology. During his presentation, he walked us through the Scrum process and provided some scenarios where this methodology could serve to be more effective than the traditional Waterfall methodology. One key takeaway from his presentation was when he shared a real-life example of a project that was run using Scrum methodology. He explained how his team was able to adapt to changing client requests and keep everyone on track through daily meetings and backlogs via using Scrum methodology. Beyond understanding the methodology, this helped me highlight the importance of being flexible and responsive when working on difficult projects. Another key takeaways from his presentation was the importance of communication and collaboration. Scrum promotes a culture of transparency and accountability, creating an environment where people provide support to those in need. This can help prevent the miscommunication, that is often an unintended consequence of Waterfall methodology. Overall, Cesar's presentation was incredibly insightful and valuable. It helped me deepen my understanding of Agile and Scrum methodology and inspired me to seek out more opportunities to learn from experts in the field.

Donald James

Donald James, a former employee of NASA, was among the notable guest speakers at the event. He shared his insights through his book, Manners Will Take You Where Brains and Money Won't. As the title suggests, his speech emphasized the importance of manners. Although I was familiar with the significance of a handshake, Mr. James' speech provided a new perspective by highlighting the value of genuine encounters and the true essence of good manners. By recounting his experiences and how going beyond a simple handshake created several relationships, I learned how Mr. James' manners helped him find purpose and satisfaction in his workplace. As someone who spent 35 years in public affairs at NASA, Mr. James' wisdom informed me about the significance of displaying dignity and etiquette in every interaction. Observing how he built meaningful relationships with his colleagues and others around him made me realize the importance of having good manners and approaching every encounter with authenticity and enthusiasm.



Trishala Jain

During her presentation, Ms. Jain, a former student of the DEDA program, shared valuable insights on how to merge business and engineering interests. As someone who is passionate about both technological innovation and empowering others, I have always believed that having a solid foundation in both fields is crucial. However, finding programs and opportunities that cater to these diverse interests can be challenging. Ms. Jain's talk was a game-changer for me as she introduced me to the MET/EECS program at UC Berkeley, where students can obtain two degrees in just four years. Additionally, she provided me with valuable resources to pursue my interests individually, such as attending start-up conventions and high-school internships. Her presentation helped me identify new paths to explore after high school, which has made the transition to college less daunting. Furthermore, meeting Ms. Jain, a successful DEDA program alumna, helped me understand how the skills learned in this program can translate into work opportunities and life beyond high school. As a college speaker, she also gave me valuable insights into the college application process and the different types of degrees available at UC Berkeley. Overall, Ms. Jain's talk was an excellent source of inspiration and guidance, and I'm grateful for the opportunity to have learned from her.



Clorox In-Person Tour

I had the opportunity to tour the Clorox factory in Pleasanton where I got to visit their scaled-down laboratories and learn about their production line and day in the life of someone who works at Clorox. I got to see how Clorox conducts its experiments, whether they be larger in scale or smaller variations. We often overlook the minute details in our everyday products, not giving a second-thought about the chemistry behind our cleaning products. Prior to this tour I did not anticipate the thought and experimentation required to ensure that clorox wipes retained their moisture without dripping onto the table or absorbing completely into the cloth. I underestimated the amount of testing required to create a strong cleaning agent that is not toxic or harmful to our skin. Additionally, I was unaware of the far-reaching spread of the Clorox company. I always associated Clorox with cleaning products without looking into subsidiary companies they own such as Burt’s Bees, Glad and Nature’s Products.



Oracle Jobshadow

The oracle job shadow was a unique virtual field trip that provided me with the opportunity to meet numerous professionals at Oracle via breakout rooms. Initially we all attended a presentation conducted by VP of Engineering where we learnt about their job responsibilities as well as his journey to engineering. After this presentation we were broken down into groups that rotated breakout rooms allowing us to meet everyone from engineers to those leaning towards sales. This opportunity allowed me to explore different career paths and learn the importance of goal-setting. Numerous presenters shared a sense of communication and goal-setting, explaining the importance of developing communicative skills early to ensure strong team-work and creating personalized goals to ensure growth and prevent feelings of imposter-syndrome. Meeting with these professionals also provided me with insight into the working world, providing me with the glimpse of skills and pathways in the workforce industry.
